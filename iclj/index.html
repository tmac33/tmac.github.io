The educational difference between NZ and CHN

The composition of study time is the biggest difference between western and Chinese education systems. For example, in the undergraduate study, the learning time of a course is 20 hours per week, which may mean that the teacher’s lecture time will reach 20 hours. The teacher will tell you that you have a complete understanding of the course system and content, so as to ensure that students’ learning level is meet. In contrast, for a course of western education, the teacher may only talk for 2 hours, and the remaining 18 hours require students to master the system and structure of knowledge by themselves, which requires a high level of autonomy in learning.

How do you incubate an innovation program

Project incubation is a complex system that involves innovation, management, collaboration, marketing, communication, etc. Specific to each employee generally only responsible for their own specific part.The factors that influence the innovation of enterprises are internal and external. How to do a good job in the cultivation of enterprise innovation is the first point learned. There are three levels of innovation: incremental, semi radical, and radical. Different costs paid by different enterprises have different impacts on the fate of enterprises, which is reflected in PTW/PNTL. How to make innovation happen in your company is a lively course.

a reflective approach to me

A concrete experience

When I am getting a research topic， I am eager to answer the following questions: who, what, where, when, why and how. If i adopt this approach, the first four questions will help me sort out what happened and the final two questions will help with the next stage of reflection.

Reflection

At this stage we are interested in finding out what the really significant actions and words were and we will be answering the questions ‘why’ and ‘how’ did these things happen.

Generalisation

The key question that I will want to answer here is ‘so what?’ In going through the concrete experience and reflection stages I have, in effect, revealed ‘what’ happened, so now I need to work out what should be the impact of those events on my future actions.

Action

There are just two questions that i need to ask myself as i take action: l. What do I want and what do I think I can achieve? 2. How will I know that I’m on the way?
